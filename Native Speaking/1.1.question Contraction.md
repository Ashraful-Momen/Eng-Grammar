# 🔥 1. YES / NO QUESTIONS (Most Common)

### Do / Does

* Do you like it? → **D’ya like it?**
* Do you need help? → **D’ya need help?**
* Does he work here? → **Does he work here?** (less reduction)

---

### Did

* Did you eat? → **Didja eat?**
* Did you finish? → **Didja finish?**
* Did you call him? → **Didja call ’im?**

---

### Are / Is / Am

* Are you ready? → **Are ya ready?**
* Are you coming? → **Are ya coming?**
* Is he here? → **Is he here?** (clear usually)

---

### Can / Could

* Can you help me? → **Can ya help me?**
* Can you do it? → **Can ya do it?**
* Could you wait? → **Couldja wait?**

---

### Will / Would

* Will you come? → **Will ya come?**
* Would you like it? → **Wouldja like it?**

---

# 🔥 2. WH- QUESTIONS (VERY IMPORTANT)

### What

* What are you doing? → **Whatcha doing?**
* What do you want? → **Whaddya want?**
* What did you say? → **What’d ya say?**
* What is that? → **What’s that?**

---

### Where

* Where are you going? → **Where ya going?**
* Where do you live? → **Where d’ya live?**
* Where did you go? → **Where’d ya go?**

---

### Why

* Why are you late? → **Why ya late?**
* Why did you do that? → **Why’d ya do that?**

---

### When

* When are you coming? → **When ya coming?**
* When did you start? → **When’d ya start?**

---

### Who

* Who are you talking to? → **Who’re ya talking to?**
* Who did you meet? → **Who’d ya meet?**

---

### How

* How are you? → **How are ya?**
* How do you know? → **How d’ya know?**
* How did you do that? → **How’d ya do that?**

---

# 🔥 3. “GOING TO / WANT TO” QUESTIONS

* Are you going to go? → **Are ya gonna go?**
* Do you want to eat? → **D’ya wanna eat?**
* Are you going to work? → **Are ya gonna work?**

---

# 🔥 4. TAG QUESTIONS (VERY COMMON IN REAL TALK)

* You’re coming, right?
* You like it, don’t you?
* He’s here, isn’t he?
* We can go, can’t we?
* You finished, didn’t you?

👉 Fast speech:

* **You’re coming, right?**
* **Nice, isn’t it?**

---

# 🔥 6. INDIRECT QUESTIONS (Polite / Formal)

* Can you tell me where he is?
* Do you know what time it is?
* Could you tell me how this works?
* Do you know why he left?

👉 Spoken fast:

* **D’ya know where he is?**
* **Can ya tell me what time it is?**

---

# 🔥 7. NEGATIVE QUESTIONS (IMPORTANT FOR IELTS)

* Don’t you like it?
* Didn’t you see that?
* Aren’t you coming?
* Can’t you do it?

👉 Spoken:

* **Don’tcha like it?**
* **Didn’tcha see that?**

# 🔥 1. SHOULD (Advice / suggestion)

### Basic

* Should I go? → **Should I go?** (usually clear)
* Should we start? → **Should we start?**

### Fast / casual

* What should I do? → **What should I do?** (sometimes → *What sh’ I do?*)
* Should you call him? → **Shouldja call ’im?** (fast speech)

---

# 🔥 2. COULD (Ability / polite request / possibility)

### Questions

* Could you help me? → **Couldja help me?**
* Could I use this? → **Could I use this?**
* What could we do? → **What could we do?**

### With “have” (very common)

* Could have → **could’ve → coulda**

Example:

* What could you have done? → **What coulda you done?**

---

# 🔥 3. WOULD (Very common in speaking)

### Questions

* Would you like some tea? → **Wouldja like some tea?**
* What would you do? → **What wouldja do?**

### With “have”

* would have → **would’ve → woulda**

Example:

* What would you have done? → **What woulda you done?**

---

# 🔥 4. SHOULD HAVE / WOULD HAVE / COULD HAVE

These are **EXTREMELY common in daily speech**

* Should have → **should’ve → shoulda**
* Could have → **coulda**
* Would have → **woulda**

Examples:

* What should I have done? → **What shoulda I done?**
* You should have told me → **You shoulda told me**
* I could have helped → **I coulda helped**

---

# 🔥 5. SHALL (British usage mostly)

👉 Less common in American English, but still important (especially for IELTS)

* Shall we go? → **Shall we go?** (clear)
* What shall we do? → **What shall we do?**

👉 In real speech, natives often replace it with:

* **Should we go?**
* **Wanna go?**

---

# 🔥 6. MUST (obligation / deduction)

* Must I go? → **Must I go?**
* You must be tired → **You must be tired**

### Fast speech

* Must have → **must’ve → musta**
* He must have left → **He musta left**

---

# 🔥 7. MIGHT / MAY (possibility)

* Might we try again? → **Might we try again?**
* May I come in? → **May I come in?** (formal)

### Fast speech

* Might have → **might’ve → mighta**

Example:

* He might have forgotten → **He mighta forgotten**

---

# 🔥 8. CAN / CAN’T (VERY IMPORTANT)

* Can you help? → **Can ya help?**
* Can I go? → **Can I go?**
* Can’t you see? → **Can’tcha see?**

# ⚠️ Important reality (very important)

* Natives **don’t always reduce everything**
* Words like **should, shall, must** are often **spoken clearly**
* Reductions happen mostly with:

  * **you → ya**
  * **have → ’ve / a**
  * **did you → didja**
