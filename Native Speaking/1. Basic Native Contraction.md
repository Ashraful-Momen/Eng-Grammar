You’re on the right track—this is exactly the kind of training that boosts your real-life listening and helps a lot in the IELTS.

I’ll give you a **large, practical list** covering:

* Daily conversation reductions
* Question forms (very important for listening)
* Real-life patterns natives use

---

# 🔥 1. Common Reductions (Daily Use)

### Basic verbs & helpers

* going to → **gonna**
* want to → **wanna**
* have to → **hafta**
* got to → **gotta**
* need to → **needa**
* used to → **usta**

---

### Pronoun + verb combinations

* let me → **lemme**
* give me → **gimme**
* tell him → **tell ’im**
* tell them → **tell ’em**
* ask her → **ask ’er**
* help him → **help ’im**

---

### Common phrases

* kind of → **kinda**
* sort of → **sorta**
* a lot of → **alotta**
* out of → **outta**
* because → **’cause**
* probably → **probly**
* I don’t know → **I dunno**

---

# 🔥 2. Question Forms (VERY IMPORTANT)

### “Do you” → **d’ya / doya**

* Do you like it? → **D’ya like it?**
* Do you want to go? → **D’ya wanna go?**

---

### “Did you” → **didja**

* Did you eat? → **Didja eat?**
* Did you call him? → **Didja call ’im?**

---

### “Are you” → **are ya**

* Are you ready? → **Are ya ready?**

---

### “What are you” → **whatcha**

* What are you doing? → **Whatcha doing?**

---

### “What do you” → **whaddya**

* What do you want? → **Whaddya want?**

---

### “Where are you” → **where ya**

* Where are you going? → **Where ya going?**

---

### “Why are you” → **why ya**

* Why are you late? → **Why ya late?**

---

### “How do you” → **how d’ya**

* How do you know? → **How d’ya know?**

---

### “When are you” → **when ya**

* When are you coming? → **When ya coming?**

---

# 🔥 3. Linking Sounds (Super Important)

### Consonant + vowel linking

* pick it up → **pickitup**
* take it → **takeit**
* turn it on → **turniton**

---

### T sound changes (American-style)

* better → **bedder**
* water → **wadder**
* little → **liddle**

---

### Dropping sounds

* next day → **nex day**
* friends → **frenz**
* months → **munts**

---

# 🔥 4. “Have” Contractions (Daily speech)

* I have → **I’ve**
* you have → **you’ve**
* should have → **should’ve**
* could have → **could’ve → coulda**
* would have → **would’ve → woulda**
* must have → **musta**

Example:

* I could have done it → **I coulda done it**

---

# 🔥 5. Negative Contractions

* do not → **don’t**
* did not → **didn’t → dinnit (fast)**
* cannot → **can’t**
* will not → **won’t**
* is not → **isn’t**
* are not → **aren’t**

---

# 🔥 6. Real Conversation Sentences

### Daily speaking style

* What are you doing? → **Whatcha doing?**
* I am going to eat → **I’m gonna eat**
* Do you want to come? → **D’ya wanna come?**
* Give me a minute → **Gimme a minute**
* I don’t know → **I dunno**
* Let me check → **Lemme check**
* You have to try this → **You hafta try this**

---

# 🔥 7. Fast Native Questions (IELTS GOLD 🔑)

These are VERY common in listening:

* **D’ya wanna go?**
* **Whatcha doing?**
* **Didja see that?**
* **Where ya going?**
* **How d’ya know?**
* **Why ya here?**
* **You gonna come?**
* **You gotta minute?**
* **Can ya help me?**

---

# ⚠️ Important truth (don’t ignore)

* These forms are **ESSENTIAL for listening**
* But:

  * ❌ Don’t write them in exams
  * ❌ Don’t overuse in formal speaking

---

# ✅ How to practice (simple but powerful)

Daily 15 min:

1. Pick 10 phrases
2. Read normal form
3. Say reduced form
4. Make 1 sentence
